## 6. Challenges and Limitations

Despite the impressive accomplishments of the hello-Jules project, it is important to acknowledge the challenges and limitations of AI-powered code generation. While AI agents are capable of generating high-quality code, they are not yet able to replicate the creativity and intuition of a human developer. They are also limited by the data they are trained on, which can lead to biases and blind spots. For example, an AI agent that is trained on a dataset of code that is predominantly written by men may be more likely to generate code that reflects a male perspective. This could have a number of negative consequences, such as the creation of software that is not accessible to women, or the perpetuation of harmful stereotypes.

Furthermore, the use of AI in software development raises a number of ethical and societal questions. Who is responsible for the code generated by an AI agent? If an AI-generated program causes harm, who is to blame? The developer who used the AI? The company that created the AI? The AI itself? These are complex questions that will require careful consideration as we move forward into the age of AI-powered software development. Another challenge is the issue of job displacement. As AI agents become more capable, it is possible that they will be able to perform many of the tasks that are currently performed by human developers. This could lead to significant job losses in the software industry, and it is important to have a plan in place to address this issue. This could include retraining programs for displaced workers, as well as the creation of new jobs that are focused on the development and management of AI systems.
